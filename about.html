About Me, Lucy.

I was introduced to yoga through my mother, who ran a small studio at the end of our garden. My first training (Yoga Alliance 200 hours) was in Hatha yoga at a traditional school in Goa, India.

These days, I am privileged to teach Hatha (holistic combinations of individual postures/practices), Restorative (gentle, therapeutic emphasis), and Vinyasa Flow (flowing, sometimes strong).

My teaching vocabulary is, in turn, influenced by my professional certifications in Embodied Yoga Principles (yoga for self development), Thai Yoga Massage, and Restorative Yoga.

I also have a vested interest in combining Body Positivity with yoga. I am aware that many people come to yoga hoping that it will change their physical appearance (which it will), and that this goal needs to be shaken off in order to reach the more fulfilling goal of accepting and loving ourselves as we are. Some of my writings on the mind-body connection have been published in Om Magazine, Yoga International (9/10 from over 1,400 reviewers), and Sivana East. I also have a free e-book, published with Embodied Yoga Principles.

Finally, as an MRes student at the Royal College of Art, I specialise in applying yogic principles to Communication Design methods. For my undergraduate degree, I read Philosophy, French and Spanish at Durham University, obtaining a First Class while captaining a women's football team and working as a freelance travel writer.

My journey with yoga is ongoing. I am currently enrolled on a two-year Vajrasati course (Yoga Alliance 500+ hours) with a couple of wise senior teachers who keep reflecting light onto my path.

But that's enough about me. How could I help you?
